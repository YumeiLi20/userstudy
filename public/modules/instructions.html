Instructions

Hello, for the next while you will be completing some trials.

What are these trials?

Your goal is to click as many red dots as possible, once a red dot is clicked a new one will pop up somewhere on the screen. You must click as many dots as you can within 5-10 seconds. After that 5-10 seconds you will be prompted with a screen where you will click the boxed number representing how many blue squares you saw on the screen.

How do I tell what's what?

Your cursor is a crosshair that moves with your mouse, whenever your crosshairs is over a dot/square that dot/square will turn green even if it's the red dot, so be sure to click it even if it is no longer red!

How do these techniques work?

Each technique has a different freeze region, except when you must use no technique or freezing the whole screen. When you hit 'Shift' the dots/squares inside the highlighted region displayed on screen will freeze, expect for freezing the whole screen which has no highlighted region (freezes whole screen on freeze). The techniques will be explained below.

NOTE: Once a red dot is frozen and it's original goes off screen you will only have 2 seconds to click it.

Will I be able to test the techniques before using them?

Of course! Before using any new technique/trail combination you will be allowed practice. You may practice as much as you want and during you will be told how many blue squares you guessed correctly!

NOTE: Practice at least 4 times so you can get an idea of the various speed/density combinations you will encounter with each technique/trail combination!

Techniques

Freeze Whole Screen

The freeze region for this technique is the whole screen and therefore no highlighted region will be displayed.

Freeze Around Cursor

The highlighted region for this technique is a circle created around your cursor.

Freeze Around Closest

The highlighted region for this technique is a circle created around the closest dot/square to your cursor.

Freeze Trajectory

The highlighted region for this technique is a cone/flashlight like region drawn in the direction of your cursors movement.
